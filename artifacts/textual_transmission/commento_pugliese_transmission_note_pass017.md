# Textual Transmission Note: Pugliese and the Commento

- Artifact ID: `transmission_commento_pugliese_pass017`
- Type: textual transmission note
- Status: SOURCE_ANCHORED_DRAFT
- Evidence status: high

## What Changes

Pugliese does not replace our controlled Italian TEI text of Pico's *Commento*, but she makes the edition-history problem sharper. Benivieni should be treated as a participant in the Commento's transmission, not just as the poem's author. He publishes, frames, translates, and sometimes alters Pico-related texts.

## Commento Implications

The claim that Benivieni changed Pico's treatise to soften criticism of Ficino directly supports Allen's account of suppression, softening, and the later concealment of Pico-Ficino disagreement. This means future Commento summaries should tag not only doctrinal claims but also possible mediation by Benivieni, especially where Ficino is named, implied, attacked, or softened.

## Method Rule

For every Commento passage involving Ficino, Venus, love, beauty, Mind, soul, or Platonic exegesis, record:

- whether the passage is likely part of Pico's hard anti-Ficinian argument,
- whether later Benivieni/Buonaccorsi publication history may soften it,
- whether Allen or Pugliese supplies a transmission warning,
- whether the passage should be compared against the Italian TEI, Stanley, Jayne, and any available early print or manuscript witness.
