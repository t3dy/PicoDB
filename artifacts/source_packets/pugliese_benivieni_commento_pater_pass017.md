## Core Finding

Olga Zorzi Pugliese's article gives PicoDB important context for the *Commento*, but its main new textual discovery concerns another Pico work: Benivieni's previously unknown vernacular translation of Pico's *Expositio singularis in Orationem Dominicam*.

## New Information for the Commento

Pugliese strengthens the collaborative frame around Pico and Benivieni. She treats Benivieni not merely as the poet whose canzone Pico commented on, but as a close friend, collaborator, textual mediator, translator, and posthumous promoter of Pico's writings.

For the *Commento sopra una canzone d'amore*, Pugliese adds or reinforces these points:

- The *Commento* belongs to a broader working partnership between Pico and Benivieni, not a one-off exercise.
- Pico wrote the commentary in 1486, with possible later revisions.
- The canzone is identified with the difficult vernacular `de amore carmen` that Pico describes in a 1486 letter as full of the mysteries of ancient secret philosophy.
- Benivieni's poem and Pico's prose do not fully coincide doctrinally; the poem functions partly as pretext or occasion for Pico's independent theory of love.
- Benivieni's later self-commentary may have been stimulated by Pico's explanatory model and by Lorenzo de' Medici's earlier self-commentary.
- Benivieni helped diffuse Pico's works by publishing Pico's Latin elegy and the explanation of the canzone in his own works.
- Most important for the Ficino problem: Pugliese says Benivieni introduced changes into Pico's treatise in order to soften the criticism of Ficino's theory of love.

## New Adjacent Pico Text

The article makes PicoDB add the *Expositio singularis in Orationem Dominicam* / commentary on the *Pater noster* to the primary-text acquisition queue. Pugliese identifies Benivieni's unpublished Florentine vernacular translation in Laurenziana MS Fondo Acquisti e Doni 55 and notes related witnesses at Tours and Toronto.

## Research Consequences

This source should feed three areas:

- Commento transmission: Benivieni is a mediator who may revise, soften, and reframe Pico.
- Ficino/Pico essay: the softening of anti-Ficinian criticism gains another source anchor.
- Late Pico piety: the Pater Noster commentary becomes an acquisition priority alongside letters, devotional writings, and Savonarolan/posthumous reception.
