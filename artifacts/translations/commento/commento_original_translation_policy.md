# Translation Policy: Pico's Commento

- Artifact ID: `translation_policy_commento_original_english`
- Type: translation policy
- Status: ACTIVE
- Source text: `Pico_Commento_BibliotecaItaliana_TEI_bibit000827_xml_2e50ef08`

## Principle

This project can produce its own English translation of Pico's Italian *Commento* because the underlying Renaissance Italian work is public domain and PicoDB has a controlled Italian TEI witness. The translation must be made from the Italian witness, not reconstructed from Stanley or Jayne.

## Witness Roles

- Italian TEI: controlling source for the translation.
- Stanley 1651/Gardner 1914: early modern reception witness and vocabulary comparison.
- Jayne 1984: restricted modern translation witness for page-level collation only.

## Translation Rules

- Translate from the Italian sentence and argument structure whenever possible.
- Preserve technical vocabulary with a glossary note when needed: `essere causale`, `essere formale`, `essere participato`, `mente`, `anima razionale`, `natura angelica`, `felicita`, `amore`, `bellezza`, `idea`.
- Keep Pico's source names visible: Plato, Plotinus, Proclus, Porphyry, Dionysius, Aristotle, Avicenna, Hermes, Zoroaster, Moses, Kabbalists, Talmudists.
- Do not silently Christianize, Ficinianize, or modernize Pico's claims.
- Prefer readable scholarly English over archaic imitation.
- Add translator's notes for doctrinal ambiguity, source problems, or places where the Italian is syntactically compressed.
- Collate difficult passages against Stanley and Jayne when available, but never let either English version replace the Italian.

## Coverage Plan

1. Paratexts: title, Bonacursius dedication, Benivieni to the reader.
2. Benivieni's canzone.
3. Book I, chapters 1-13.
4. Book II, chapters 1-24.
5. Book III, chapters 1-4.
6. *Commento particulare*, stanza by stanza.
