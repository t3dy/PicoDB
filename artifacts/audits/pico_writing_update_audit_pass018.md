## Executive Finding

The writing system has reached the point where earlier drafts need a controlled revision pass rather than more isolated additions. Passes 014-017 changed the project in five global ways:

1. The *Commento sopra una canzone d'amore* is no longer a primary gap. PicoDB now has a controlled Italian TEI witness, a public-domain early English translation/reception witness, a restricted modern Jayne witness record, and an original-translation protocol.
2. The *Commento* must be read as a Ficino dispute text, a love-metaphysics text, and a textual-transmission problem. Allen gives the Ficino frame; Pugliese adds independent evidence that Benivieni mediated and softened the anti-Ficinian edge.
3. Benivieni is no longer merely the author of the canzone. He must be tagged as Pico's collaborator, poetic occasion, posthumous promoter, possible textual reviser, vernacular translator, and Florentine reception agent.
4. The Pater Noster commentary / *Expositio singularis in Orationem Dominicam* becomes a major new primary-text acquisition gap, especially because Pugliese identifies Benivieni's unpublished vernacular translation and manuscript witnesses.
5. The next close-reading system needs revision fields: stale claim, new source anchor, affected artifact, revision priority, governing scholar, ontology update, and website destination.

## Essay-by-Essay Audit

### Ficino Dispute Essay

Priority: highest.

What to add:

- A full Benivieni mediation section: Benivieni as the one who both occasions the *Commento* and later participates in its publication/softening.
- A Pugliese-Allen convergence: Allen shows the dispute with Ficino; Pugliese adds textual-transmission evidence that Benivieni softened Pico's criticism of Ficino's love theory.
- A controlled-witness appendix: Italian TEI, Stanley/Gardner, Jayne restricted witness, and future early-print/manuscript witnesses.
- A distinction between Pico's prose argument and Benivieni's canzone. The poem can be the occasion for Pico's independent love theory without being identical to it.
- A chapter on the politics of posthumous harmony: later readers receive a softened Pico-Ficino relation, so the essay should explain how conflict becomes concord in reception.

### Commento Reading Plan and Section Summaries

Priority: highest.

What to add:

- Convert the reading plan from "control access" to "collation and close-reading execution."
- Add a Commento witness matrix: TEI Italian, Stanley/Gardner, Jayne, Basel/early print, manuscript or edition leads.
- Add fields to each section summary: Ficino agreement/correction, love doctrine, Venus function, beauty doctrine, Mind/soul/body hierarchy, poem-commentary relation, mediation risk, translation status.
- Add a warning that shell-visible mojibake in older extracted filenames/titles should not be used as a textual base; translation should return to the TEI/source text.

### Biography

Priority: high.

What to add:

- Benivieni as friend, collaborator, editor/promoter, translator, religious companion, and witness to the late Florentine/San Marco orbit.
- Pugliese's evidence that Benivieni helped diffuse Pico's work, defended him against charges, and may have had some exposure to Hebrew/Kabbalistic interests around Pico.
- The Pater Noster commentary as a late-piety and devotional-exegesis gap, not only a bibliographic footnote.
- A note that Pico's posthumous image is shaped by mediators: Gianfrancesco, Benivieni, Savonarolan circles, early printers, and translators.

### Kabbalah Synthesis

Priority: medium-high.

What to add:

- A cautious Benivieni reception paragraph: Pugliese's evidence for Benivieni's interest in Hebrew/Kabbalistic materials, his exclusion from a Mithridates lesson, and later traces such as a Latin-Hebrew dictionary or possible horoscope attribution.
- A transmission warning: Christian Kabbalah is not only a source problem inside Pico's texts; it is also a reception problem among friends, translators, and editors.
- Do not make Pugliese a source for Pico's Kabbalah itself. Use Busi and Wirszubski for source control; use Pugliese for Benivieni's reception and mediation.

### Astrology Essay

Priority: high.

What to add:

- Pugliese's Benivieni episode from the *Disputationes*: Benivieni appears as an interlocutor in a discussion of whether stars influence terrestrial life.
- A paragraph on Benivieni initially accepting a Ficinian idea that Pico rejects. This sharpens the early/late development story and the difference between Pico, Ficino, and the Florentine poetic circle.
- The possible horoscope attribution to Benivieni should be cataloged as reception/attribution evidence, not promoted as a secure primary fact without more controls.

### Angelology Essay

Priority: low-medium.

What to add:

- No major direct angelology claim from Pugliese.
- Add a limited late-devotional context note: the Pater Noster commentary may matter for Pico's prayer, grace, inner ascent, and mental devotion, but it should not be forced into angelology until the text is acquired.
- The essay still needs the 900 Conclusions angelology table promised in pass 011.

### Heptaplus and Biblical Hermeneutics

Priority: medium-high.

What to add:

- Pater Noster as an adjacent devotional-exegetical text: not part of the *Heptaplus*, but relevant to Pico's biblical and prayer-centered interpretation after the Roman crisis.
- A "Mosaic exegesis vs. prayer exegesis" comparison placeholder: *Heptaplus* reads Genesis cosmologically and anagogically; Pater Noster likely reads a liturgical prayer as inner religious practice.
- Benivieni's vernacular translation should be flagged as a reception bridge from Latin Pico to Florentine devotional readers.

### Being and Unity / Aquinas / Dionysius

Priority: medium.

What to add:

- No major direct Pugliese addition.
- Add a methodological cross-reference: the same Pico who disciplines Ficinian Platonism in *On Being and Unity* also contests Ficino's love theory in the *Commento*.
- Use the Pater Noster gap only cautiously, as possible evidence for late theological language once acquired.

### Platonism and Aristotelianism

Priority: high.

What to add:

- The *Commento* should become a central case of contentious co-Platonism: Pico uses Platonic love discourse while correcting Ficino.
- Add Benivieni's canzone as poetic occasion, not doctrinal master-text.
- Add "controlled instability" at the level of reception: posthumous softening can make real dispute appear as harmonious concord.

### Neoplatonist and Arabic Authors in the 900

Priority: medium.

What to add:

- The essay remains underdeveloped and needs author-cluster tables more than new prose.
- Add a bridge to the *Commento*: the `de amore carmen` described as difficult and full of ancient secret philosophy can help frame Pico's non-900 use of Neoplatonic/esoteric source registers.
- Keep the main work on Proclus, Plotinus, Iamblichus, Arabic intellect traditions, and thesis clusters.

### Theses According to Pico's Own Opinion

Priority: high, but separate from Pugliese.

What to add:

- Build the promised thesis table. The draft is still mostly a program.
- Add an audit field for "related later prose work" so own-opinion theses can be linked to *Commento*, *Heptaplus*, *On Being and Unity*, *Apology*, and *Disputationes*.
- Add transmission risk only when a thesis is mediated through edition history or later reception, not because of the Pugliese article directly.

### Missing Writings / Next Steps

Priority: highest.

What to add:

- Correct the stale claim that the *Commento* is the highest open primary gap. It is now acquired in a controlled Italian witness, though not yet fully summarized/collated.
- Promote the Pater Noster commentary and Benivieni translation to the highest new acquisition priority.
- Add manuscript leads from Pugliese: Laurenziana Fondo Acquisti e Doni 55, Tours MS 2110, Toronto Fisher/Buchanan, and early print/translation witnesses.
- Add a status distinction: "acquired but needs close-reading" versus "identified but not acquired."
