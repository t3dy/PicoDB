MELANCTHON'S REPLY TO G. PICO DELLA MIRANDOLA

                 BY QUIRINUS BREEN
   This Reply (1558) was written as an answer to Pico's famous let-
ter (1485)  to Ermolao Barbaro.'  Melancthon had long been inter-
ested in the Pico-Barbaro correspondence about philosophical literary
style.2 The Reply is " in behalf of Barbaro," whose answer to Pico he
desired to improve upon.
   Melancthon's subject is the praise of eloquence or rhetoric, and an
attack upon the unrhetorical discourse of the scholastics.  While he
makes no claim to be a philosopher he is strongly devoted to a concept
of wisdom which is quite at variance with Pico's, and partly at vari-
ance with Barbaro's.  Pico had wanted to divorce rhetoric from wis-
dom. Barbaro had wanted to make wisdom express itself rhetorically.
Melancthon subordinated wisdom (or philosophy)  to rhetoric.  To
Melancthon philosophical wisdom pertains to practical life.  He sees
nothing in Pico's claim that certainty of knowing can be attained
through contemplation and through logic.  This, in turn, is associated
with his doctrine of man. The ideal man is not the contemplator but
the orator.  It is apparent that his thinking lies in the Isocratean tra-
dition rather than the Platonic or Aristotelian.  Just as he subordi-
nates philosophy to rhetoric he also subordinates theology to it.
